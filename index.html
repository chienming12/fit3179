US CAR ACCIDENTS FROM 2016-2021

This visualization shows the statistics of Car Accidents that occured in the United States from 2016-2021 and the reasons and results of the car accidents are shown in the visualization.

The Total Cases of Car Accidents in the United States from 2016 to 2021 is shown on the left, where the cases of Car Accidents grow each year. This is due to the advancement of our current technology bounded world as more and more cars are being produced, hence more and more cars are purchased by users which causes a rise in car accidents as well. However, this causes them to be less careful and mindful while driving as cars get cheaper.

With that said, 2021 has a huge jump in car accidents as more people are eager to get out of their houses due to Covid-19 lockdowns. The only place that does not involve crowds would be the personal space in your own car. The mental state of the general public has went downhill due to the lockdown which might be a reason to the sudden skyrocket of car accidents in 2021.

The chart on the right shows the Top 3 States of the United States with the Highest Car Accidents Cases, which are California, Texas, and Florida. These 3 states are relatively larger in size and are more developed states in the United States. Thus, more citizens have access to cars which leads to more car accidents as well.

From the map above, we can see that California, Texas, and Florida all have relatively high humidity. This shows that places with higher humidity are more prone to car accidents as humid weather would cause more slipper roads.

Although the highest humidity states are not shown in the top 3 states, it is due to the size of the state. California, Texas, and Florida are considered larger states and when compared to other similar sizes states, the number of cases of these 3 states are much higher, which humidity holds a part of the reason for this.

The chart above shows how weather affect the number of cases of accidents. In this case, we can see that on a Fair weather, most drivers are not mindful as driving becomes a muscle memory movement, and fair weather makes the environment feel safe. Thus, people tend to be more confident in speeding or driving without much focus. Obviously, this is also due to people travelling more on a fair weather.

On the other hand, Rain weather causes most drivers are to be more careful as they know that there is a risk of accidents happening, hence they drive safer and slower. Once again, the reduce of accidents is also caused by people travelling lesser on a rain weather.

The chart on the left shows that most accidents are not very severe, with a measurement of 1 to 4, most accidents occur on Severity 2, which is not so severe. This is because we can see from the previous chart that most accidents happen on a fair day, thus drivers that encounter accidents can usually lessen the severity as the roads are rougher and easier to brake, causing less damage to the accidents.

The chart on the right shows the Severity against the Distance of Car Accidents, which proves the previously mentioned point where Severity 2 has the most cases which is proven by the distance of the car accidents. The amount of cases in Severity 2 mostly only has a Distance < 50 meters. This means that most accidents happen dont travel far, which is why it is less severe. Although there are far distances in Severity 2 as well, this proves that even if the distance of the accident is huge, it is not so sever due to the fair weather, where drivers can have better control of their car.
